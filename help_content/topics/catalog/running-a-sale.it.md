This guide walks you through setting up the most common types of sales using the promotions system. Whether you need a sitewide percentage discount, a targeted category sale, or a time-limited flash sale, follow these step-by-step examples to get your promotion live quickly.

![Sitewide Sale Setup](/static/core/admin/img/help/running-a-sale/sitewide-sale-setup.webp)

## Sitewide Percentage Sale

A sitewide percentage sale is the simplest and most popular promotion type — for example, "20% off everything." Here is how to set one up using the 5-step promotion wizard.

1. Navigate to **Marketing > Sales & Promotions** and click **Create Promotion**.
2. **Step 1 — Basics**: Enter a clear name such as "Summer Sale — 20% Off Everything." Add an optional description for internal reference. Leave the **Active** checkbox checked so the promotion goes live on its start date.
3. **Step 2 — Discount**: Select **Percentage Off** as the discount type and enter **20** as the value.
4. **Step 3 — Schedule**: Set the **Start Date** and **End Date** for your sale period. The promotion will automatically activate and deactivate on these dates.
5. **Step 4 — Products**: Select **All Products** (the default). This ensures every product in your catalog receives the discount.
6. **Step 5 — Review**: Verify all your settings — name, discount type, percentage, schedule, and product scope. When everything looks correct, click **Create Promotion**.

Your sitewide sale is now scheduled and will apply automatically when the start date arrives.

## Category Sale

A category sale targets a specific department or product group — for example, "30% off Outerwear." This is ideal for seasonal clearance or department-specific promotions.

Follow the same wizard steps as a sitewide sale, with one key difference in Step 4:

1. In **Step 4 — Products**, select **Categories** instead of All Products.
2. The **Available Categories** panel appears with a search field. Find and add the categories you want to include in the sale (e.g., "Outerwear", "Jackets").
3. Only products within those categories will receive the discount.

This approach works well for end-of-season clearance, rotating department promotions, or highlighting a specific product line.

### Choosing the Right Targeting

The promotions system offers several targeting options. Use this table to decide which one fits your sale:

| Targeting | Best For |
|-----------|----------|
| **Categories** | Department-wide sales (e.g., all shoes, all electronics) |
| **Brands** | Brand-specific promotions (e.g., "Nike Week") |
| **Collections** | Curated groups (e.g., "Summer Essentials", "Gift Ideas") |
| **Products** | Hand-picked individual items |

## Flash Sale

A flash sale is a short-duration, high-discount promotion designed to create urgency — typically lasting 24 to 48 hours with 40-50% off.

1. Create a new promotion following the standard wizard steps.
2. In **Step 2 — Discount**, set a compelling discount (e.g., 40% or 50% off).
3. In **Step 3 — Schedule**, set a tight date window — for example, starting Friday at 00:00 and ending Saturday at 23:59.
4. In **Advanced Settings**, set a high **Priority** value (e.g., 10). This ensures the flash sale overrides any ongoing lower-priority promotions that might also apply to the same products.
5. Pair the flash sale with an **announcement bar** (found under Marketing > Announcements) to display a countdown or urgency message across your storefront.

### Flash Sale Tips

- **Keep it short** — 24 to 48 hours maximum. Scarcity drives action.
- **Offer a meaningful discount** — flash sales should feel special compared to your regular promotions.
- **Promote across channels** — announce via email, social media, and your storefront announcement bar simultaneously.
- **Monitor stock** — high-discount flash sales can move inventory quickly, so keep an eye on stock levels.

## Seasonal and Holiday Sales

Major shopping events like Black Friday, Christmas, and summer sales require advance planning and sometimes layered promotions.

### Planning Ahead

Schedule your seasonal promotion well in advance by setting a future start date days or weeks before the event. This gives you time to prepare marketing materials, test the promotion, and make adjustments without rushing.

### Layering Promotions

Consider layering multiple promotions for maximum impact. For example:

- Create a **sitewide 20% off** promotion with **Priority 5**
- Create a **40% off Clearance** category promotion with **Priority 10**

The higher-priority category promotion will apply to clearance items, while the sitewide promotion covers everything else. This lets you offer deeper discounts on specific products without manually excluding them from the broader sale.

## Individual Product Sales

For discounting a single product quickly, you can use the product-level sale instead of creating a full promotion. This is built directly into the product editing form.

1. Navigate to **Products > All Products** and click on the product you want to put on sale.
2. Open the **Pricing** tab.
3. In the **Sale** section, configure the following:
   - **Sale Type** — Choose from Percentage Off, Amount Off, or Fixed Price.
   - **Sale Value** — Enter the discount amount or percentage.
   - **Start Date / End Date** — Optionally schedule when the sale begins and ends. Leave these empty for an immediate, indefinite sale.
4. Save the product.

Product-level sales are simpler and faster to set up, but they only apply to one product at a time.

### When to Use Each Approach

| Approach | Best For |
|----------|----------|
| **Promotion** | Multiple products, categories, or brands at once |
| **Product Sale** | Quick discount on a single product |

If you need to discount more than a handful of products, a promotion is almost always the better choice — it keeps your discounts organized in one place and is easier to manage.

## Tips

- **Plan ahead** — schedule promotions in advance rather than activating them last-minute. This gives you time to verify everything works correctly.
- **Test first** — check the affected products count shown in the Review step of the wizard to verify the promotion targets the right products before it goes live.
- **Use clear names** — "Summer Sale 20% Off" is much easier to find and manage later than "Promo 1" or "Test discount."
- **Remove when done** — deactivate or delete expired promotions to keep your promotions dashboard clean and avoid confusion.
- **Announce it** — pair your promotions with announcement bars and email campaigns for maximum visibility. The best discount in the world does nothing if your customers do not know about it.
