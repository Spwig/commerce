Product trust badges appear on your product detail pages to build customer confidence before they add items to their cart. You can configure **two separate sets** of badges — one for physical products and one for digital products — so the messaging is relevant to what the customer is buying.

## Accessing the Badge Editors

1. Navigate to **Settings > Design & Theme** to open the Design Dashboard.
2. Click **Page Templates** to open the Template Configuration page.
3. Scroll down past the Product Page Template section to find the two badge editors:
   - **Product Trust Badges** — for physical product pages (Classic, Full Width, and Gallery Focus templates).
   - **Digital Product Trust Badges** — for digital product pages (Digital template).

## Physical Product Badges

These badges appear on products using the **Classic**, **Full Width**, or **Gallery Focus** templates. They are displayed below the Add to Cart button on the product page.

The default badges are:

| Icon | Text |
|------|------|
| Truck | Free Shipping |
| Returns | 30-Day Returns |
| Shield | Secure Payment |

These defaults are suitable for most physical goods stores. Customise them to match your actual policies — for example, if you offer 14-day returns instead of 30, update the badge text accordingly.

## Digital Product Badges

These badges appear on products using the **Digital** template, which is designed for software, ebooks, courses, and other downloadable products. They appear in the product info section.

The default badges are:

| Icon | Text |
|------|------|
| Lightning | Instant Delivery |
| Infinity | Lifetime Access |
| Shield | Secure Download |

Digital products have different customer concerns than physical goods — buyers want to know they'll receive their purchase immediately and can access it long-term.

## Why Two Separate Sets?

Physical and digital products have different selling points:

- **Physical products** — Customers care about shipping cost, return policies, and payment security.
- **Digital products** — Customers care about instant access, download security, and license terms.

Having separate badge sets lets you address each audience's concerns directly, rather than showing irrelevant badges like "Free Shipping" on a software download page.

## Adding a Badge

1. Click the **+ Add Badge** button in the relevant section.
2. Select an icon from the dropdown.
3. Enter the badge text (up to 60 characters).

Each section supports a maximum of **6 badges**.

## Editing a Badge

Each badge row contains:

- **Drag handle** — For reordering via drag and drop.
- **Icon preview** — Shows the currently selected icon.
- **Icon dropdown** — Choose from over 30 available icons.
- **Text field** — The label displayed on the product page.
- **Delete button** — Removes the badge.

## Reordering Badges

Drag badges by their handle to rearrange them. The display order on the product page matches the order in the editor.

## Saving Changes

Click **Save Configuration** at the top or bottom of the page after making changes. A confirmation message confirms the save was successful. Changes apply immediately to all product pages.

## Available Icons

The icon dropdown includes icons suited to both physical and digital contexts:

- **Security** — Lock, Shield, Shield Check, User Shield, Key
- **Quality** — Check Circle, Certificate, Award, Medal, Star
- **Satisfaction** — Heart, Thumbs Up, Handshake
- **Payment** — Credit Card, Money, Percent
- **Shipping** — Truck, Fast Shipping, Returns, Exchange
- **Support** — Support (headset), Phone, Email, Clock
- **Digital** — Lightning, Download, Infinity
- **Other** — Gift, Globe, Leaf (Eco), Recycle

## Tips

- Match badges to your real policies. Saying "Free Shipping" when you charge for delivery erodes trust rather than building it.
- For physical products, highlight your strongest selling points — free shipping, generous returns, and secure payment are the most effective.
- For digital products, emphasize immediate access and long-term value. "Lifetime Access" and "Instant Delivery" address the two biggest concerns digital buyers have.
- Keep text short and scannable. Badges should be understood at a glance.
- Three to four badges per set works best. Fewer than two feels sparse; more than five feels excessive.
- Remember that badge text is displayed as-is — if your store serves multiple languages, consider using the admin translations feature or keeping badge text simple enough to be universally understood.
